Color Naming in Two Languages

Kyle Thayer, Younghoon Kim , Gabriella Gorsky
Probabilities of color names, given particular colors in English and Korean.

In data visualization, one of the most critical considerations for designers is that of color. Previous studies explored color perception by relating with cultures and languages. We aim to explore color naming differences by collecting color naming data from a broader range of languages. To gather these data, we ran a short-term pilot study on LabintheWild (www.labinthewild.org). We obtained 10428 color names of 14 languages, but only English(8832) and Korean(864) were gathered enough to be analyzed. Paired-samples analyses revealed a significant difference between the English and Korean ratios for approximately 71% of pairwise comparisons. And we visualized the data to present their differences.
